Overview

Project duration: 2.5 months Role: level design, play testing, modeling, lighting, audio.

Mauerfall is a stealth-adventure video game, in where the player takes on the role of Olivia, a 14-year old girl who is living in East Germany during the Berlin Crisis in 1961.Pursued by the East German police, Olivia must escape East Germany in order to reunite with her mother in West Berlin, which is blockaded by the Berlin Wall.

Tools: Unreal engine 4, Maya, photoshop, after effects, Audacity.

Narrative

14 year old Olivia and her father wish to reunite with her mother in West Berlin, now surrounded by the Berlin Wall. They must tread carefully through the streets of East Berlin, as guards are on the lookout for anyone wishing to escape East Germany. They must act quickly though, as failed and successful attempts by other escapees are resulting in stronger reinforcements to the Wall, making it more difficult to escape.

Mechanics

The core loop involves the player constantly running and avoiding the secret police using the city landscape or artifacts such as cars and garbage cans as they make their way across each level. Getting caught by the police or failing to reach the Berlin wall in two days results in a game over, resetting the player to the last save location. Getting to the wall within two days is the winning end condition. Time acts as a non-renewable resource represented by the day/night cycle; a drain that is incrementally consumed independent of the player’s actions. Throwing objects are a limited resource that the player can interact with and reuse, i.e. thrown again and again. The player is able to interact with certain NPCs such as the secret police and characters that can be saved from the police. The game view isthird-person isometric.

Level Design

Levels were initially sketched on paper as proof on concept while experimenting with different layouts. Promising level were then translated on grid paper to provide a sense of scale. The grid paper also helped define areas of negative and positive space, along with specific location of various artifacts.

AI

While walking through the streets of Berlin, NPCs loyal to the policies of East Germany can report the player to the police allowing for dynamic enemy algorithms rather than static patrol points. Groups of AI can try and flank the player when they are within their line of sight (applies during day and night).During the night cycle, the AI can also track the player by sound. The more quickly the player moves, the more sound will be made from footsteps. If a sound is heard by the AI they will go investigate at the origin of the sound.

Design

Mauerfall takes inspiration from several games such as Metal Gear Solid and Mark of the Ninja for our mechanics, and Volume, Unfinished Swan, Mirror’s Edge for aesthetics. The stealth genre is nothing new and some of the basic mechanics such as hiding behind objects is a common element seen in this genre. Mauerfall takes elements from various different games and incorporates them into an original, fictional narrative with semi historical accuracy.

As a team, we always try to justify our design choices. For example the protagonist, Olivia doesn’t wish to do harm to anyone. Therefore, we decided that it was appropriate that she doesn't have a means of fighting back such as using weapons like knives or guns.

Mauerfall is a game that focuses both on narrative and mechanics. The gameplay duration is a main selling point of Mauerfall. Games such as The Last of Us and Portal while excelling in both narrative and mechanics, tend to consume more time. Compared to those games, Mauerfall is an inexpensive alternative that has an engaging story and interesting mechanics.
